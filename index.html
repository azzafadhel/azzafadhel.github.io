ICML 2024Offline OptimizationSurrogate Modeling
Learning Surrogates for Offline Black-Box Optimization via Gradient Matching
Problem

Many scientific and engineering design tasks require optimizing expensive black-box objectives—materials, molecules, proteins, robot designs—using only a fixed offline dataset of past evaluations. Standard regression surrogates yield unreliable gradients outside the data regime, causing gradient-based search to move toward poor designs.

Approach

We developed MATCH-OPT, which constructs monotonically improving trajectories from offline data and trains the surrogate with a gradient-matching objective based on line integrals along those trajectories—encouraging reliable search directions for downstream optimization.

Results

Evaluated on diverse DesignBench tasks (robot morphology, locomotion, superconductors, DNA design), MATCH-OPT achieved the best overall mean normalized rank across strong baselines including COMS, ROMA, BONET, CBAS, CMA-ES, and ensemble surrogates.

My role: Co-designed the MATCH-OPT algorithm and led major parts of the PyTorch implementation—trajectory construction, custom loss, surrogate training, candidate optimization, benchmark evaluation, and empirical analysis. Contributed to writing method and experimental sections; released code for reproducibility.

AAAI AISI 2026Scientific MLMaterials Discovery
Nanoporous Materials Discovery via Search Bias-Guided Surrogate Modeling
Problem

Discovering high-performing COFs and MOFs for carbon capture, methane storage, and hydrogen storage is difficult—each wet-lab evaluation is expensive, and scientists typically have only a small historical dataset alongside a large pool of unevaluated candidates.

Approach

I led development of OptReg, a surrogate-guided discovery framework that trains neural surrogates using both value prediction and an optimization-bias regularizer derived from monotonically improving sequences in the offline dataset—encouraging candidates to be ranked by discovery potential rather than prediction accuracy alone.

Results

Evaluated on three real-world tasks (COF methane storage, MOF gravimetric H₂ capacity, MOF volumetric H₂ capacity), OptReg achieved strong improvements over the best offline material: 125.8% on COF(NG) and 309.1% on MOF(UV). Best-in-class on COF(NG) and MOF(UV) vs. MSE surrogates, one-step BO, batch BO, CMA-ES, and diffusion models. Feature-attribution analysis identified pore geometry, density, and void fraction as key structural drivers.

My role: Drove the project end-to-end—problem formulation, algorithm design, surrogate training, candidate-ranking experiments, baseline comparisons, feature-attribution analysis, and paper writing.

AISTATS 2026Meta-LearningGaussian Processes
Black-Box Optimization from Small Offline Datasets via Meta-Learning with Synthetic Tasks
Problem

Many real-world optimization problems (materials discovery, molecular design, biological sequence design, hardware optimization) operate with tiny or biased offline datasets. Standard surrogates trained on such data fail to learn useful optimization directions and produce poor candidates.

Approach

I led development of OptBias, a meta-learning framework that uses Sim4Opt—a GP-based synthetic task generator—to create auxiliary functions mimicking the target objective and simulate gradient-ascent trajectories from low to high-value regions. OptBias meta-trains a surrogate on these synthetic tasks so it rapidly adapts to a real target using only a small offline dataset.

Results

Evaluated on the poorest 1% of offline data across 6 benchmarks (Ant morphology, D'Kitty morphology, TFBind8 DNA design, three RNA inverse-folding tasks), OptBias achieved the best overall mean rank (1.33) and best performance on 4/6 tasks—outperforming GA, MINs, COMs, DEMO, LTR, Match-Opt, batch BO, REINFORCE, and ExPT.

My role: Owned the project end-to-end—problem formulation, OptBias algorithm design, Sim4Opt synthetic data generation, meta-learning implementation, fine-tuning and candidate optimization pipelines, benchmark evaluation, ablation studies, and paper writing.

AAAI/IAAI 2026Deployed AIAdditive Manufacturing
Discovery of Feasible 3D Printing Configurations for Metal Alloys via AI-Driven Adaptive Experimental Design
Problem

Finding feasible DED parameters for GRCop-42—a NASA copper alloy with low infrared laser absorptivity and high thermal conductivity—is extremely expensive. The process space exceeds 100 million configurations, and each physical experiment requires machine time, materials, expert labor, and post-print quality analysis. Prior to this work, 37 configurations had been tested over several months without success.

Approach

I led development of BEAM, an AI-driven adaptive experimental design framework that models the unknown feasibility function with a probabilistic surrogate trained on past outcomes, uses an active-search acquisition strategy to select batches of promising configurations, and incorporates expert domain constraints to prune infeasible regions.

Results & Impact

Deployed in a real DED laboratory, BEAM discovered feasible configurations at 950W, 700W, 600W, and 500W using only 10 experiments per laser-power setting—and found three feasible configs at 600W within the same budget. This demonstrated, for the first time, that GRCop-42 can be printed at low laser powers (500W–1000W), making advanced aerospace alloy fabrication accessible to smaller labs and lower-cost platforms.

My role: Led the AI/ML side—problem formulation, BEAM algorithm design, surrogate modeling, acquisition strategy, domain constraint integration, experiment planning with materials-science collaborators, trial analysis, and paper writing.

ESWEEK 2026Multi-Objective BOHealthcare Wearables
Wear-DSE: Information-Theoretic Design Optimization of Wearable Systems for Cost-Effective Health Monitoring
Problem

Designing wearable health devices requires selecting sensors, processors, and communication modules while balancing latency, energy consumption, comfort, and user preferences across a massive discrete component-selection space. Brute-force design exploration is impractical because high-fidelity evaluation requires simulation, hardware prototyping, or real-world measurement.

Approach

I co-designed Wear-DSE, a preference-aware multi-objective, multi-fidelity Bayesian optimization framework. It uses a compressive-sensing-inspired surrogate that maps binary hardware selections into a compact continuous embedding, and an information-theoretic acquisition strategy that jointly selects the next design and evaluation fidelity—analytical model, simulator, or real-world measurement—based on expected information gain per unit cost.

Results

Evaluated on 4 wearable health applications with a library of 80 components and three evaluation fidelities. Wear-DSE produced stronger Pareto fronts than NSGA-II, AMOSA, continuous-GP BO, and Casmopolitan BO. Multi-fidelity optimization reduced average evaluation cost from ~9,575 to ~930—roughly 90% savings—while maintaining high-quality designs.

My role: Led the AI/ML side—surrogate modeling, acquisition strategy design, preference-aware optimization, implementation, experiments, ablation studies, and result analysis.
